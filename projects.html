Eight end-to-end Power BI builds on real Kaggle datasets, each scoped for an 8-year senior — multi-fact models, advanced DAX, performance, RLS, forecasting. Replicate them, then put the best 3 on your resume & portfolio.

ExpertE-commerce

1 · Brazilian E-Commerce analytics

Dataset: Olist Brazilian E-Commerce — Kaggle olistbr/brazilian-ecommerce (~100k orders, 9 related tables)

🎯 Business problem

A marketplace wants one view of order economics: revenue, delivery performance, seller quality, and customer satisfaction — across products, sellers, and Brazilian regions.

🧮 Why it’s senior

9 normalized tables force real modeling: orders, order_items, products, sellers, customers, payments, reviews, geolocation, category translation. You must collapse to a clean star, handle a fact at order-item grain, and a separate payments fact — a genuine multi-fact model.

🧱 Model

  • Facts: FactOrderItems (item grain: price, freight), FactPayments (payment grain).
  • Dims: Date, Customer (+ geolocation), Seller (+ geolocation), Product (+ English category via translation table).
  • Two facts share Date & Order dimensions — never relate fact-to-fact.

⚡ Key DAX

On-Time Delivery % =
VAR Delivered = CALCULATE(COUNTROWS(Orders),
   NOT ISBLANK(Orders[delivered_date]))
RETURN DIVIDE(
   CALCULATE(COUNTROWS(Orders),
     Orders[delivered_date] <= Orders[estimated_date]), Delivered)

Avg Delivery Days =
AVERAGEX(Orders,
   DATEDIFF(Orders[purchase_date], Orders[delivered_date], DAY))
multi-fact starorder-item graingeo mapsdelivery SLAreview scores
🚀 Senior twistAdd RLS by seller (a seller sees only their orders) via a dynamic role + USERPRINCIPALNAME() mapping. Add a p95 delivery time measure with PERCENTILEX.INC — averages hide the late tail.
💬 Interview pitch"I modeled 9 Olist tables into a two-fact star (order-items + payments) sharing conformed dimensions, built delivery-SLA and review measures, and secured it with per-seller RLS. The map + p95 lead time exposed which states drove late deliveries."
ExpertRetail / CRM

2 · RFM segmentation & cohort retention

Dataset: Online Retail II (UCI) — Kaggle mashlyn/online-retail-ii-uci (~1M invoice lines, 2009–2011)

🎯 Business problem

Segment customers by value and behavior (RFM), measure retention by acquisition cohort, and estimate customer lifetime value to target marketing.

🧮 Why it’s senior

RFM and cohort retention are pure advanced DAX — virtual tables, dynamic segmentation, and a cohort matrix built without a physical cohort column. This is where mid-level developers stall.

⚡ Key DAX

-- Recency / Frequency / Monetary per customer
Recency Days =
VAR Last = CALCULATE(MAX(Sales[InvoiceDate]))
RETURN DATEDIFF(Last, MAX('Date'[Date]), DAY)

-- dynamic segment using a disconnected band table + TREATAS
Segment Sales =
CALCULATE([Total Sales],
  TREATAS(VALUES(Bands[Segment]), Customer[Segment]))

Build the cohort matrix: rows = acquisition month, columns = months-since-first-purchase, values = retained customers / cohort size.

RFMcohort matrixCLVvirtual tablesdynamic segmentation
🚀 Senior twistBuild a what-if parameter for "discount %" and model its effect on projected CLV. Add a field parameter so users swap R, F, or M on the axis.
💬 Interview pitch"I computed RFM scores in DAX, segmented customers via a disconnected band table with TREATAS, and built a cohort-retention matrix entirely in measures — no physical cohort column. CLV + a what-if discount slider made it actionable for marketing."

3 · Telco customer churn

Dataset: Telco Customer Churn — Kaggle blastchar/telco-customer-churn (~7k customers, 21 fields)

🎯 Business problem

Find who churns and why. Quantify churn rate, revenue at risk, and the drivers (contract type, tenure, services) so retention can act.

Churn Rate =
DIVIDE(CALCULATE(COUNTROWS(Customers), Customers[Churn]="Yes"),

Revenue at Risk =
CALCULATE(SUM(Customers[MonthlyCharges]), Customers[Churn]="Yes")

4 · HR attrition & hierarchy RLS

Dataset: IBM HR Analytics Employee Attrition — Kaggle pavansubhasht/ibm-hr-analytics-attrition-dataset (~1.5k employees)

🎯 Business problem

HR wants attrition rate by department, role, and satisfaction — and each manager should see only their org.

🧮 Why it’s senior

The standout skill here is organizational-hierarchy RLS with PATH / PATHCONTAINS, plus field parameters for flexible analysis.

⚡ Key DAX

HierPath = PATH(Employee[EmpID], Employee[ManagerID])

-- RLS role filter: manager sees their whole subtree
PATHCONTAINS(Employee[HierPath],
  LOOKUPVALUE(Employee[EmpID], Employee[Email], USERPRINCIPALNAME()))

Attrition % =
DIVIDE(CALCULATE(COUNTROWS(Employee), Employee[Attrition]="Yes"),
        COUNTROWS(Employee))

5 · Retail sales forecasting & time intelligence

Dataset: Rossmann Store Sales — Kaggle rossmann-store-sales (~1M rows, 1,115 stores) · or Walmart M5

🎯 Business problem

Forecast store sales, compare actual vs prior year & vs forecast, and analyze the impact of promotions, holidays, and store type.

🧮 Why it’s senior

Deep time intelligence, a store hierarchy, and the architecture decisions: incremental refresh design on ~1M rows and calculation groups so you don’t write YTD/PY/YoY for every measure.

⚡ Key DAX

Sales PY = CALCULATE([Sales], SAMEPERIODLASTYEAR('Date'[Date]))
Sales YTD = TOTALYTD([Sales], 'Date'[Date])
Sales 4W MA =
VAR W = DATESINPERIOD('Date'[Date], MAX('Date'[Date]), -28, DAY)
RETURN CALCULATE(AVERAGEX(VALUES('Date'[Date]), [Sales]), W)

Use the line chart’s built-in Analytics → Forecast with confidence bands; compare to actuals.

Late Delivery % =

P95 Shipping Days =
PERCENTILEX.INC(Orders,

ExpertPerformance

8 · Big-data performance lab

Dataset: NYC Yellow Taxi (tens of millions of rows) or US Accidents — Kaggle sobhanmoosavi/us-accidents (~7.7M rows)

🎯 Goal

Not a pretty report — a performance engineering project. Take millions of rows and make a model that’s small, fast, and refreshable.

🧮 What you practice

  • Cardinality reduction: split DateTime into Date + Time, drop precise lat/long precision, remove unused columns — measure size in VertiPaq Analyzer.
  • Aggregation tables: pre-summarize by date+zone; verify the engine hits the agg.
  • Incremental refresh: partition by date so only recent data reloads.
  • DAX tuning: read Server Timings in DAX Studio; kill CallbackDataID; SE vs FE.
  • Composite model: import dims + DirectQuery/agg the giant fact.
VertiPaq Analyzeraggregationsincremental refreshDAX Studiocomposite model
🚀 Senior twistDocument a before/after: model size (e.g. 3.1 GB → 780 MB) and a slow visual’s DAX time (e.g. 4.2s → 0.4s). That single slide wins senior interviews.
💬 Interview pitch"I took a 7.7M-row dataset, cut the model ~75% through cardinality work and encoding, added aggregation tables and incremental refresh, and used DAX Studio server timings to drop a key visual from 4s to under half a second."
